NayePankh Foundation is one of the leading non-profit organizations working for the upliftment of the underprivileged. The foundation is registered under the Indian Trust Act, 1882.

Focus Areas and Programs:
1. Education: Providing free primary education, books, stationery, and tutoring support to underprivileged children in slums and rural areas.
2. Food Distribution (Feed the Hungry): Regularly distributing healthy, warm meals to homeless people, daily wage laborers, and children living on the streets.
3. Clothing Drives: Collecting and distributing wearable clothes, blankets (in winters), and footwear to needy families.
4. Hygiene and Health Awareness: Organizing campaigns to teach personal hygiene, menstrual hygiene, distributing sanitary napkins, and organizing health checkups.
5. Social Welfare: Providing relief material during natural disasters, supporting disabled individuals, and raising awareness about social issues.

Founder and Team:
The NayePankh Foundation was founded by Prashant Shukla. Under his leadership, the organization has grown to support thousands of children and families. A dedicated team of coordinators, volunteers, and partners manage daily operations.

Volunteering Opportunities:
Anyone with a passion for social impact can volunteer with us. Volunteering roles include:
- Ground Volunteers (helping in food distribution and clothing drives)
- Teaching Assistants (teaching children in our learning centers)
- Campaign Managers (helping organize health camps and awareness sessions)
To register as a volunteer, visitors should fill out the registration form. We collect their Full Name, Email, Phone Number, City, and Areas of Interest.

Internship Opportunities:
We offer internships for students looking for real-world experience in the social sector. Internships are available in:
- Social Work and Operations
- Teaching and Curriculum Design
- Social Media and Content Writing
- Fundraising and Event Management
Internships are typically 4 to 12 weeks long. Certificate of Completion and Letter of Recommendation are provided to successful interns. To apply, students need to provide their Name, Email, College, Course, Year of Study, Skills, and an optional Resume Link.

Donation Process:
We welcome contributions to help keep our programs running:
1. Monetary Donations: Donations can be made securely through our bank account or our website's payment gateway. All donations are tax-exempt under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act.
2. Book Donations: We accept storybooks, textbooks, notebooks, and stationery items for children at our learning centers.
3. Clothing Donations: Wearable clothes, sweaters, blankets, and school uniforms can be donated.
4. Food Donations: We accept dry rations (rice, pulses, flour, oil) and packaged food items for distribution.
